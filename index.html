About

Fredericton is the capital city of New Brunswick, a beautiful province on the east coast of Canada. Fredericton sits on the Saint John River, a river which opens up into the Bay of Fundy, and is notably wide. The only cities in the province that are larger than Fredericton are Moncton and Saint John. The city is home to the University of New Brunswick (UNB), one of the oldest Universities in North America (Est. 1785). Fredericton is a wonderful city with lots to offer!

Some facts about Fredericton:

  • City Population: 58, 220
  • Country Population: 37.6 Million
  • Continent: North America
  • Geography: On the Saint John River
  • Climate: Temperaters ranging -15C to 26C
  • Demographics: The average age of a person in Fredericton is 40.9 years old.
  • Area: 132.6 Square Kilometers
  • Population Density: 439.2 People per Square Kilometer

Cuisine

Dish Description Photo

Blueberries

While not everyone considers them a dish of their own, blueberries are a key cullinary element in Fredericton and the entire province of New Brunswick. Used in things such as blueberry pie, or jelly or wine, blueberries are a part of the identity of New Brunswick!

Seafood

In Fredericton and particularly on the east coast, seafood is a delicacy. While not for everyone, lobster, shrimp, and salmon are all common dishes amongst the people of Fredericton.

Potatoes

New Brunswick is known for it's potatoes. In fact, potatoes are the primary crop in the province with around 20,000 hectares of land dedicated to potato production! It is the home to the majority of McCain's potato farms, which produce the potatoes for every McDonald's in Canada.

Willie O'Ree

Willie Eldon O'Ree, was the first ever black hockey player in the National Hockey League (NHL). Born in Fredericton, New Brunswick on October 15th, 1935, O'Ree played for the Boston Bruins. O'Ree secretly played while blind in his right eye. In 2018, Willie O'Ree was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame.
